Cross-Border Finance

Cross-border trade is complex. SettleX simplifies it. This academy breaks down the mechanics of international payments, regulatory compliance, and how our platform saves you 2-3% on every transaction.

1. The Global Trade Problem

For decades, Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) in India have been paying a hidden tax on their growth. While large corporations enjoy wholesale exchange rates and instant settlements, SMEs are forced to use legacy banking rails that are slow, expensive, and opaque.

The "SME Trade Tax"

High Costs (2-3%)

Banks charge a "spread" on currency conversion. If the real rate is ₹84, they give you ₹82. On a $10,000 invoice, you lose ₹20,000 instantly.

Slow Speed (T+4)

Money takes 3-5 days to travel through multiple intermediary banks (SWIFT network), choking your cash flow.

Compliance Pain

Manual reconciliation of FIRA/FIRC documents takes weeks. Missing documents can lead to ED (Enforcement Directorate) notices.

2. How Money Moves: The Old Way

Traditional cross-border payments rely on a chain of "Correspondent Banks." It's like a game of telephone, but for money. Each bank takes a fee and adds time.

4. Trade Finance Glossary

FIRA / FIRC

Foreign Inward Remittance Advice/Certificate. A mandatory document proving that foreign currency entered India legally. You need this to claim GST refunds and prove to the government you aren't laundering money.

Nostro / Vostro Account

Latin for "Ours" and "Yours". A Nostro account is an account an Indian bank holds with a US bank (in Dollars). A Vostro is the reverse. This is the plumbing of international finance.

EDPMS

Export Data Processing and Monitoring System. An RBI database that tracks every export invoice. If a payment isn't matched ("knocked off") against an invoice in EDPMS, the exporter gets "caution listed."

EEFC Account

Exchange Earner's Foreign Currency Account. A specialized account that allows Indian exporters to hold 100% of their earnings in foreign currency for one calendar month to hedge against rate fluctuations.
